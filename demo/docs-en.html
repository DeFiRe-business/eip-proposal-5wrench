Coercion-Resistant Vault

ERC-8300 | Step-by-step guide

English Espanol

What is this?

The Coercion-Resistant Vault is a smart contract wallet designed to protect cryptocurrency holders against physical coercion attacks (known as "$5 wrench attacks"). It partitions your funds into two tiers:

  • Hot balance -- a small amount you can spend immediately, subject to a daily rate limit (e.g., 0.05 ETH per day).
  • Cold vault -- the bulk of your funds, locked behind a timelock (e.g., 5 minutes to 72 hours) and/or requiring guardian approval to unlock.

Under coercion, you can truthfully say: "I can only send you this much right now. The rest is locked and I physically cannot access it faster." The constraint is verifiable on-chain -- no deception needed.

Prerequisites

1MetaMask browser extension

Install MetaMask from metamask.io if you don't have it. It works on Chrome, Firefox, Brave, and Edge.

2Switch to Sepolia testnet

Open MetaMask, click the network dropdown (top-left), and select Sepolia test network. If you don't see it, enable "Show test networks" in MetaMask settings.

3Get Sepolia ETH

You need a small amount of Sepolia ETH (free, no real value) to interact with the vault. Get some from a faucet:

  • sepoliafaucet.com
  • cloud.google.com/application/web3/faucet/ethereum/sepolia

Paste your MetaMask address and request ETH. It usually arrives in under a minute.

Connecting to the vault

4Open the demo

Go to the demo page in the same browser where MetaMask is installed.

5Click "Connect MetaMask"

MetaMask will pop up asking you to connect. Select the account you want to use and click "Connect". The demo will automatically switch you to Sepolia if you're on a different network.

6Check your role

After connecting, the banner at the top will show:

  • Vault owner -- you can deposit, spend from hot balance, request cold withdrawals, and execute DeFi swaps.
  • Not the owner -- you can only deposit ETH into the vault and view its state. This is normal if your address is not the vault's owner address.

Understanding the dashboard

Once connected, the dashboard shows the vault's real-time state:

  • Total balance -- all ETH held by the vault contract.
  • Hot balance (orange border) -- the amount you can spend right now without waiting. This is capped by the spending limit per epoch.
  • Cold balance (blue border) -- the locked portion. Withdrawing from here requires waiting the timelock or getting guardian approval.
  • Spent this epoch -- how much of the hot budget you've used in the current 24-hour window.
  • Unlock delay -- the timelock duration for cold withdrawals (e.g., 5 minutes in the demo).

Actions: step by step

Deposit ETH

Anyone can deposit ETH into the vault, even if they're not the owner.

  1. Enter an amount in the "Deposit ETH" field (e.g., 0.05).
  2. Click Deposit.
  3. MetaMask will ask you to confirm the transaction. Click "Confirm".
  4. Wait for the transaction to be mined (usually 12-15 seconds on Sepolia).
  5. The dashboard updates automatically showing the new balance.

Hot spend (instant transfer)

Send ETH from the hot balance to any address. Subject to the daily spending limit.

  1. Enter a recipient address in the "Recipient address" field.
  2. Enter an amount within your remaining hot budget.
  3. Click Hot Spend.
  4. Confirm in MetaMask.
  5. The transfer is instant. The "spent this epoch" counter increases.
If you try to spend more than the remaining hot budget, the transaction will revert with "ExceedsHotBudget". The hot budget resets every 24 hours.

Cold withdrawal (timelocked)

Request a withdrawal from the cold vault. The funds are locked until the timelock expires.

  1. Enter the recipient address and amount.
  2. Click Request Withdrawal.
  3. Confirm in MetaMask.
  4. A new entry appears in the "Pending withdrawals" table with a countdown timer.
  5. When the countdown reaches zero, click Execute to complete the transfer.
Any guardian (or the owner) can Cancel a pending withdrawal at any time before it's executed. This is the "panic button" -- if an attacker forced you to request a withdrawal, your guardians can cancel it during the timelock window.

DeFi swap on Uniswap V3

The flagship demo: swap ETH for USDC through the vault, using real Uniswap contracts on Sepolia.

  1. Enter an amount of ETH to swap (e.g., 0.001).
  2. Click Wrap + Swap.
  3. MetaMask will ask for 3 separate confirmations:
  • Transaction 1: Wrap ETH into WETH via execute(WETH, deposit())
  • Transaction 2: Approve the Uniswap router to spend WETH via approveToken()
  • Transaction 3: Execute the swap via execute(router, exactInputSingle(...))

After all three confirm, the vault holds USDC. The activity log shows the received amount.

Key insight: The hot spending counter stays at zero during the swap. DeFi operations via execute() are value-preserving (you exchange ETH for equivalent USDC), so they bypass the spending limit. Only direct transfers via hotSpend() consume the budget.

Security model

Why this works under coercion

If an attacker forces you to transfer funds, you can only send the hot balance (a small amount). The rest is locked behind the timelock. The attacker can verify this on-chain -- you're not hiding anything. Requesting a cold withdrawal starts a countdown that gives you (or your guardians) time to cancel it after the attacker leaves.

Guardians

Guardians are trusted addresses (family, friends, institutional custodians) who can:

  • Cancel pending cold withdrawals (panic button).
  • Approve cold withdrawals instantly via multisig (bypasses timelock when 2+ guardians agree).
  • Pause the entire vault in an emergency (freezes all value-moving operations for up to 24 hours).
  • Unpause the vault before the auto-expiry (requires multisig approval).

Configuration is also protected

An attacker cannot force you to raise the spending limit and drain immediately. Limit increases are timelocked just like cold withdrawals. Only limit decreases (which make the vault more secure) take effect immediately.

FAQ

Is this using real money?

No. This demo runs on Sepolia testnet. Sepolia ETH has no real value. You can get it for free from faucets.

Why does my cold withdrawal say "ExceedsColdBalance"?

Your vault's total balance is smaller than the spending limit, so all ETH is in the hot bucket and the cold balance is zero. Deposit more ETH so the total exceeds the spending limit.

Why does the Uniswap swap fail?

The WETH/USDC pool on Sepolia sometimes has low liquidity. The ETH-to-WETH wrap step always works and proves the mechanism. If the swap fails, the activity log will show the reason.

Can I deploy my own vault?

Yes. The full source code, deployment scripts, and instructions are in the GitHub repository. You need Foundry installed and a Sepolia RPC endpoint (free from Alchemy or Infura).
